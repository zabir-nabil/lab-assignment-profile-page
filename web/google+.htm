This is what 1 trillion of stars swirling around a supermassive black hole looks like. The Andromeda Galaxy (M31) is approaching the Milky Way at approximately 100 to 140 kilometres per second and in approximately 4.5 billion years the Andromeda Galaxy and the Milky Way are expected to collide and the result will be a giant elliptical galaxy.

I took this photo from Barcelona in November 2015 over several nights using different ISO / shutter speed settings to capture the different details of the galaxy.

Technical Specs:
- Imaging Telescope: Orion 8" f/3.9 Newtonian Reflector
- Mount: Celestron AVX
- Guiding Telescope: Orion 80mm short tube refractor
- Guiding Camera: Starshoot Autoguider
- Imaging Camera: Canon 70D
- Frames:
ISO 800, 120s * 100 light, 50 dark, 40 bias, 40 flat
ISO 800, 100s * 50 light, 50 dark, 40 bias, 40 flat
ISO 640, 200s * 76 light, 50 dark, 40 bias, 40 flat
ISO 400, 100s * 50 light, 30 dark, 30 bias, 30 flat
- Software: BYEOS > Pixinsight > Lightroom
